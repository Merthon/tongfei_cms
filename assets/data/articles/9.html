The Urgent Need for Standardization of Energy Storage Cooling Systems

In recent years, with the advancement of the "dual carbon" goals, the transformation of the global energy structure and the rapid development of renewable energy, the scale of energy storage project construction has been continuously expanding. Energy storage stations play an increasingly important role in ensuring the stable operation of power grids and improving energy utilization efficiency.

Against the background of rapid industry development, the demand for battery cell capacity and system energy density has also increased. Even with large-capacity cells, the construction of a 100-megawatt energy storage project still requires hundreds of thousands or even hundreds of thousands of cells combined, which will generate a lot of heat during operation, thus putting higher requirements on the temperature control management of energy storage systems.

If the cooling system is not standardized, it may not only affect the safe operation of the energy storage station, but also reduce the service life of the energy storage system. Therefore, it is imperative to formulate a scientific and reasonable cooling system specification. As a key supporting equipment for battery energy storage stations, the water cooling system has been widely used in the market, and the corresponding water cooling products should be more standardized to meet more and larger market demands.

The Emergence of the Group Standard

To promote the standardized development of energy storage station cooling systems and improve the overall level of the industry, the Standardization Working Committee of China International Economic and Technological Cooperation Promotion Association took the lead.

Drafting units including TONFY, Xuji Electric Co., Ltd., Chaowei Power Group Co., Ltd., and Power Grid Planning and Research Center of Guizhou Power Grid Co., Ltd., as well as relevant industry experts, jointly initiated the drafting and compilation of the group standard "Specifications for Multi-Level Cooling Systems of Energy Storage Stations".

This standard will comprehensively regulate the design, manufacturing, installation, commissioning, operation and maintenance of cooling systems, providing strong guarantee for the safe and stable operation of energy storage stations.
